The Local Challenge

The Silent Thief of Our Province's Harvests

In our province, smallholder farmers lose up to 15% of their harvest to spoilage and improper storage. For our local marginalized farming families, this isn't just a statistic—it's the difference between turning a profit and falling into debt.

  • Traditional cold storage is too expensive for our local farmers
  • Existing IoT solutions require stable internet, which remote barangays lack
  • Our province's most remote, off-grid communities are left completely behind

How It Works

Technology That Adapts to Local Farmers

Edge AI (TinyML)

Our machine learning models run directly on the device, analyzing temperature and humidity patterns to predict spoilage without needing internet connectivity.

  • • Local data processing
  • • Real-time predictions
  • • No cloud dependency

Long-Range LoRa

LoRa radio technology enables communication across kilometers of rough provincial terrain, perfect for remote farming barangays.

  • • 5-10km range in rural areas
  • • Ultra-low power consumption
  • • Penetrates physical obstacles

Solar Power System

Integrated solar panels with battery backup ensure continuous operation even during cloudy days and provincial power outages.

  • • 72-hour battery backup
  • • Weather-resistant design
  • • Zero electricity costs
Our Roadmap

From Pilot Barangay to Provincial Scale

We are starting local to prove our impact, with a clear path to covering our entire province.

1

Local Pilot & Calibration

Deploying in our home barangay to calibrate TinyML models for local crops and prove measurable post-harvest loss reduction.

2

Provincial LGU Rollout

Partnering with our Provincial Agriculture Office and local cooperatives to subsidize and distribute units to neighboring municipalities.

3

Province-Wide Network

Establishing a decentralized network of off-grid data nodes across the province, creating a real-time map of local crop storage health.
